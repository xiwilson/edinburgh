Why Edinburgh?

Edinburgh, Scotland's Capital

Edinburgh, the capital of Scotland, is a city that effortlessly blends history, culture, and breathtaking scenery. Nestled among rolling hills, its skyline is dominated by the majestic Edinburgh Castle, perched atop Castle Rock, offering panoramic views of the city below. The Old Town’s cobbled streets and medieval closes transport visitors back in time, while the elegant Georgian architecture of the New Town exudes a timeless charm. Arthur’s Seat, an ancient volcano and the highest point in Holyrood Park, provides a stunning natural retreat, rewarding hikers with unparalleled views of the city. Whether it’s the enchanting glow of the Christmas markets, the world-famous Edinburgh Festival Fringe, or the mesmerizing spectacle of the Royal Military Tattoo, the city’s vibrant atmosphere captivates all who visit.

Beyond its beauty, Edinburgh is steeped in history and storytelling, inspiring some of the world’s most beloved literary works. From the haunted vaults beneath the city to the winding streets that influenced J.K. Rowling’s Harry Potter, Edinburgh is a place where myths and legends intertwine with reality. The Royal Mile, stretching from Edinburgh Castle to the grand Palace of Holyroodhouse, is lined with historic landmarks, charming pubs, and hidden closes waiting to be explored. The city’s rich cultural heritage, world-class museums, and thriving arts scene make it a haven for history lovers, creatives, and explorers alike. Whether wandering through the picturesque Dean Village, indulging in traditional Scottish cuisine, or enjoying the warm hospitality of its people, Edinburgh leaves a lasting impression on all who experience its magic.

Attractions

Top things to do in Edinburgh

Mary King's Close

Hidden beneath Edinburgh’s Royal Mile, Mary King’s Close is a fascinating underground network of preserved 17th-century streets and homes. Once a bustling hub, it was sealed off and shrouded in mystery, giving rise to eerie tales of plague victims and ghostly hauntings. Today, guided tours take visitors deep into its darkened passageways, offering a spine-tingling glimpse into the lives of Edinburgh’s past residents, making it a must-visit for history lovers and paranormal enthusiasts alike.

Location

2 Warriston’s Close, High Street, Edinburgh, EH1 1PG

Why it's amazing

This is an experience like no other, a must-do for anyone visiting Edinburgh!

Edinburgh Castle

Towering over the city atop Castle Rock, Edinburgh Castle is one of Scotland’s most iconic landmarks. Steeped in history, it has witnessed battles, royal ceremonies, and dramatic sieges for over a thousand years. Inside, visitors can explore the Crown Jewels of Scotland, the Stone of Destiny, and the atmospheric Great Hall. With breathtaking views and tales of kings, queens, and warriors, the castle remains a symbol of Scotland’s rich and resilient heritage.

Location

The Esplanade, Edinburgh, EH1 2NG

Why it's amazing

One of the most beautiful castles in the world sits right on my doorstep. Yes, I am a little biased, but you cannot deny this stunning structure and it's history.

Holyrood Palace

The official residence of the British monarch in Scotland, the Palace of Holyroodhouse stands at the end of the Royal Mile, exuding grandeur and history. Once home to Mary, Queen of Scots, the palace has played host to royal drama, intrigue, and political power struggles. Visitors can explore its lavish state apartments, the hauntingly beautiful ruins of Holyrood Abbey, and the exquisite gardens, all set against the dramatic backdrop of Arthur’s Seat.

Location

The Palace of Holyroodhouse,, Edinburgh,, Edinburgh, EH8 8DU

Why it's amazing

As a glorified history geek, Holyrood Palace ticks all the boxes and oozes with Scottish and British history.
